Before the portrait, the sketch.

Moments from the real world.

Two baristas move around the espresso machines with the pace and grace of modern choreography. Between them stands a repairman that has generic coffee before he leaves home. He ignores the rush and affront around his presence as a stationary center pole in front of the broken dishwasher. Carefully and slowly he wraps blue tape, cutting another small piece with his teeth, around a hose the diameter of a thin straw to relieve the baristas from washing dishes.

...

Late Saturday afternoon. Leaving the grand theater that gives him pride only when he name drops to a stranger, he stopped to talk to a colleague sitting near the door. They trade excuses of paperwork, of staying, of duties. Both stand ready to leave, heating up under heavy coats, putting off even delaying.

...

The wave of response of surprise, then delight, at a squirrel running across the outfield at the premium baseball stadium, oblivious yet perfectly in tune with where he is to be.

...

Rushing down a street, ignoring storefronts, ads, names, numbers, a word grabbed her attention without slowing her pace. The meaning tumbled in her mind, following her. Then opened into a new space of possibility that had not been there.

...

It is spring, and the soil will hold no record of the hands pressing the seeds into the wet, dark earth.
