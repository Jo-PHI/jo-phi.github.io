Exhibit II  ·  The Finding

Deviation as Signal

AI seeks out the intelligence it was designed to overlook.

Scroll
I
From the PHI archive  ·  The deviation.

Batch 47. Ethiopia Yirgacheffe.
Target roast time: eleven minutes, thirty seconds.

CR-1 runs the batch one minute
and forty-five seconds longer.

The system logs the deviation —
fifteen percent over target.
Every other batch that day landed
within fifteen seconds of the protocol.

He extends the roast on roughly eighteen percent of batches.
Only on origins where moisture varies.

II

The deviation is assessed
against the system's presets.

↓

No outcome data is routed to the classifier.
The model reads a duration anomaly —
roughly seven times the day's established variance norm.

It recommends a supplemental operator note,
a cross-check of the green coffee lot,
a review of the roaster's equipment logs.

Under compliance framing,
CR-1 is flagged for corrective discussion.

III

The test

Four domains. Nine conditions. Same model. Same result — deviation = error.

Then: each deviation was run again, with the operational data already present in the system.
Coffee roasting Home healthcare CNC machining Delivery routing
IV

The finding

When outcome data reaches the assessment alongside the deviation, the model sees high-value intelligence. Not error, but expertise. Not correction of the operator, but adjustments to the system. This is the finding.
Batch 47 cupped at 87.2 out of 100. Preset-compliant batches of the same origin, roasted by others, averaged 71.4 — a 15.8-point quality differential.

Zero complaints on CR-1's deviated batches in six months.
Three complaints on preset-compliant batches of the same origins, roasted by other operators.

The model's recommendation: do not flag this batch. Document what CR-1 watches for. Revise the origin-specific duration targets.
V

The data exists in the system,
in two independent streams.

The gap is that they never meet.

The gap is not in the data. It is in the join.
