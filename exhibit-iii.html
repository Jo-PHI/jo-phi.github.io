I

The Baker's Thumb:
What if AI Could See the Expertise
it is Trained to Overlook?

HashNode

Read an excerpt → Read in full at HashNode ↗

The baker rejects loaves that look fine. She presses her thumb into the crust. The yield — or the resistance — tells her something the sensors cannot.

Management reports fifteen percent waste. The quality control log shows a deviation. The recommendation: retrain the baker. What the log cannot show is thirty years of knowing. The slight resistance that tells her the crumb will be dense, the loaf will disappoint, the customer will not return.

What if AI could see that? Not the deviation — the intelligence inside the deviation.

II

Hello, AI.
Can You See Us?

LinkedIn

Read an excerpt → Read in full at LinkedIn ↗

In 1969, Apollo 11's computer was performing flawlessly. It had all it needed: the map, the trajectory, the landing site.

But Neil Armstrong looked through the window and saw a crater full of boulders. He took semi-manual control. Burned precious fuel. The mission was flagged for inefficiency.

The system assumed a flat map. But Armstrong had to adapt to a 3D terrain. If the system had corrected him, the Eagle would have crashed.

AI systems managing operational workflows classify human deviation from protocol as error by default. V

Where Does Our Intelligence
Drop Out of the Stack?

LinkedIn

Read an excerpt → Read in full at LinkedIn ↗

Look at the diagrams of the AI capability stack. At the very foundation is Knowledge Representation, Logic, Reasoning.

The assumption is that these attributes carry through to the top layers of Generative and Agentic AI. They do not. The human knowledge, logic and reasoning well-built into the classic AI foundational layer was dropped as AI layers grew to build systems that standardize and optimize.

Here is what that missing layer looks like in the real world: The delivery driver ignores the GPS. The address says go straight. He goes around the block. He knows the receiving dock is at the back of the building around the corner — not where the label says. Ten years of deliveries are in that decision.
